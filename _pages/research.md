My research focuses on wearable and implantable bioelectronics, with an emphasis on soft bioelectronic interfaces, conductive hydrogels, epidermal sensors, and physiological monitoring systems.

## Paintable Epidermal Electrodes

I developed paintable on-skin dry electrodes that can form conformal, breathable, and robust bioelectronic interfaces directly on the skin. These electrodes enable long-term wireless ECG, EMG, and EEG monitoring, as well as human-machine interaction through electrophysiological signal decoding.

**Related publication:**  
**W. Zhang**, X. Xin, Y. Wang, et al. *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences*, In Press, 2026.

## Hydrogel-Based Sweat Biosensors

I developed a hydrogel-based sweat chloride sensor for non-invasive biochemical monitoring. The device uses concentration-difference-induced open-circuit voltage to achieve high sensitivity, low hysteresis, rapid response, and accurate on-body sweat chloride analysis during exercise.

**Related publication:**  
**W. Zhang**, X. Zhang, A. Dutta, et al. *Biosensors and Bioelectronics*, 288, 117805, 2025.

## Conductive Hydrogels for Bioelectronic Interfaces

I am interested in the design of PEDOT:PSS-based conductive hydrogels for wearable and implantable bioelectronic interfaces. My work focuses on improving conductivity, mechanical robustness, tissue adhesion, environmental stability, permeability, and long-term interface reliability.

**Related publication:**  
**W. Zhang#**, Y. Yang#, S. Zhang, et al. *Advanced Materials*, Under Review, 2026.

## Soft Sensors and Multimodal Sensing

I have worked on soft sensors with decoupled sensing mechanisms and iontronic pressure sensors with high sensitivity over broad sensing ranges. These platforms are useful for physiological monitoring, motion tracking, and human-machine interfaces.

**Related publications:**  
R. Yang, A. Dutta, B. Li, **W. Zhang**, et al. *Nature Communications*, 14, 2907, 2023.  
R. Yang, **W. Zhang**, N. Tiwari, et al. *Advanced Science*, 9, 2202470, 2022.

## Wearable Healthcare Devices

My research also includes wearable healthcare systems for disease monitoring and therapy, including wearable electronic devices for glaucoma monitoring and treatment.

**Related publication:**  
**W. Zhang**, L. Huang, R. N. Weinreb, H. Cheng. *Materials & Design*, 212, 110183, 2021.
